What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a place where people turn ideas, food, and coffee into magic (a.k.a. code). People with different experiences and technical backgrounds work together in teams to code a solution to a specific problem. These solutions generally take the form of mobile apps, robots, and websites. More broadly defined, a hackathon is a multi-day event where people come together, ideate, and work intensively on ideas and products to change the world!

Who can attend?

Everyone is welcome! This includes immigrant rights activists, allies, DACA recipients, and anyone interested in using their skills to protect and empower the immigrant community. Whether you’re building your first website, barely learning how to code, learning Photoshop, or hacking with an Oculus Rift, UndocuHacks welcomes you!

How much will it cost?

Admission is free for all hackers as long as you live in the United States. We will cover all costs - that includes food, lodging, and transportation.

Where will I sleep?

For those coming in from out of town, we will be providing room and lodging but we recommend you bring a sleeping bag and basic toiletries so you can hack all night long and not be too far from your team!

But I know nothing about coding?!

If you don't know nothing about coding or engineering, not to worry - learning is the objective! UndocuHacks will have workshops and pair teams with mentors in the tech industry to offer some guidance! You will never be alone in the process! :-)
